How bettongs feed

Bettongs are nocturnal and spend the day resting in nests made of grass and leaves. At night, they forage for food, using their keen sense of smell to locate fungi, roots, tubers, seeds, and insects. They dig with their strong forelimbs and sharp claws to unearth underground food sources. Bettongs play a crucial role in their ecosystem by dispersing fungal spores through their droppings, which helps maintain healthy soil and plant life.
